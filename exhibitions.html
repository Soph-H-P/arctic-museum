Exhibitions

Are you a young person looking to learn more about science? Come on down to our museum, there’s plenty to see and do. Our exhibits are designed to be accessible for interested minds, so make sure you come ready to learn and explore.

Cosmology

Physics

Explore the wonders of our cosmos. Our fantastic exhibition, ‘The Sky Above Us’, explores the night sky and what we can see and know about the universe around us. We’ll locate the various constellations and galaxies that can be seen and learn a bit about the early navigators who used the stars to travel by.

Follow the journey of our solar exploration: from early Arab traders, to Galileo’s telescope, to the latest exploration of the planets in our solar system.

Evolution

Biology

What better way to learn than to look at nature around us. The local wildlife room will teach you all there is to know about evolution and adaptation. Find out why the arctic fox is only white in the winter, why the moose has those funny antlers and how the animals in the arctic circle survive the cold.

You can also take some time to meet a few of the creatures who didn’t survive, including our Woolly Mammoth, Plesiosaur and Ichthyosaur, all fossils found locally so you’ll see what once roamed the land right where you are.

Molecules of Life

Chemistry

Ever wondered what all those elements really are. In the Molecules of Life exhibition you will get a chance to look at all the elements that make up our world.

Take on the periodic table challenge and see if you can build up the wall of elements. If you manage it there will be a chance to go up on our Chemistry wall of fame!

Join our researchers in the lab and find out what explodes and what doesn’t. Don’t worry safety goggles are provided.

Technology

Physics

The information revolution is here and robotics and artificial intelligence are the science of the future. From useful home applications of AI to industrial uses of robotics, the future is here. You can even say hello and shake the hand of Rob the Robot.

Try out the latest in virtual reality technology and explore far off lands.

Use our 3D printer to make your drawings come to life, who would have thought you would be making your own souvenirs?

Snow Discovery

Physics

Ever wondered why all snowflakes are different? Or if that is even true?

Come on over to the Snow Discovery exhibit where you will get the chance to collect and study snowflakes first hand. Learn how they form and much more.

You will also be able to find out why some snow is good for making snowballs and some isn’t. Hint it has something to do with temperature.

Ecology

Biology

As we learn more and more of the impact humans are having on the planet, the more important it becomes for scientists explore how to create a sustainable future not just for humans but for the whole planet.

In our ecology section of the museum, we unpack the dangers we currently face including global warming, extinction of species, and pollution. Looking at two of our local beauties, the Sea Eagle and Steppe Eagle you’ll learn how one species can thrive while the other continues to decrease in numbers.

This is just a tiny selection of our exhibition spaces, we have currently over 1000 exhibits to see and interact with at the Arctic Science Museum Tromsø. Come join us and see for yourself.
